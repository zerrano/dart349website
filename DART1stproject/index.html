Censorship

"Censorship is the suppression of speech, public communication, or other information, on the basis that such material is considered objectionable, harmful, sensitive, or "inconvenient. " Censorship can be conducted by governments, private institutions, and corporations." -Wikipedia

Annoying, isn't it?

Welcome to censorship, the concept that makes you hate black boxes, and pixelated faces. Censorship at the core, is nothing more than a cover, a filter, or anything that can be used to mask/distort the form of information in fear of repercussions. Censorship goes as far back as 399 BC, when the Greek philosopher, Socrates, while defying attempts by the Greek state to censor his philosophical teachings, was accused of collateral charges related to the corruption of Athenian youth and sentenced to death by drinking poisonous hemlock. Censorship is a form of control that dates way back to a much more oppressive era that continues to get frowned upon as the generations that pass by, deem it outdated and unessesary.
